Exercises on finding MLE numerically

EXERCISE:  Generate a data set of size 10 from Gamma$(\alpha,p)$ with pdf $$ f(x,\alpha,p) = \left\{\begin{array}{ll}\frac{p^ \alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)} x^{\alpha-1}e^{-p x}&\text{if }x>0\\0&\text{otherwise.}\end{array}\right.. $$ For any given $\alpha>0$ find the MLE of $p$ analytically. Use this to obtain MLE of $p$ and $\alpha $ when both are unknown. [Hint: The R functions digamma and trigamma compute first and second derivatives of $\log \Gamma (x).$]

EXERCISE: (Logistic regression) We have data $(x_1,Y_1),...,(x_n,Y_n),$ where $x_i$`s are fixed and known, and $Y_i$`s are independent $Bern(F(\alpha + \beta x_i)),$ where $F(x)$ is the logistic CDF: $$ F(x) = \frac{1}{1+e^{-x}},\quad x\in{\mathbb R}. $$ Find MLE of $\alpha, \beta$ based on simulated data.

EXERCISE:  We have a categorical data set with four categories: $A$, $B$ $C$ and $D$ with probabilities given by $p^2+q^2$, $r^2$ $2pq + pr$ and $pr+2qr,$ where $p+q+r = 1,$ and $p,q,r\geq 0.$ If the frequencies of the categories are $354, 234, 213$ and $56$, respectively, obtain MLE of the parameters.
