Exercises on frequency domain analysis of time series

EXERCISE:  Prove that the $P$ matrix is indeed unitary. This will also show that the set $\{\v f_1,...,\v f_n\}$ is indeed an ONB, as claimed.

EXERCISE:  Let $z_1,...,z_n\in{\mathbb C}$ be the DFT of the time series $x_1,...,x_n.$ . Prove that $\sum |z_i|^2 = \sum x_i^2.$ This is one form of Parseval's identity.

EXERCISE:  DFT expresses a time series in terms of sinusoidal frequencies that are integral multiples of $\frac{2\pi}{n},$ where $n$ is the lenth of the time series. Now suppose that the original time series (of length 1000) is itself a pure sinusoid with frequency $45.4\times \frac{2\pi}{1000}.$ Guess how its DFT should look like. Check your guess (theoretically or computationally). What you will see is sometimes called power leakage.

EXERCISE:  Consider the partition of the $4\times 4$ DFT matrix as given in the notes. It was mentioned that this DFT is essentially same as two $2\times 2$ DFT`s. Make this precise.

EXERCISE:  Show that a $6\times 6$ DFT is essentially made of two $3\times 3$ DFT`s.
