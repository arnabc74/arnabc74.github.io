Geometric algebra with J

Geometric algebra is an algebra, i.e., a vector space together with a product operaion that combines two vectors to produce a new vector. This product is called the geometric product. It satisfies all the things that you'd expect a product to satisfy except commutativity, and existence of inverse. It is associative, distributes over addition (both ways), allows scalars to slip out, has an identity element. Throughout most of Geonetric Algebra we keep the explicit definition of the geometric product hidden.

The vector space is of dimension $2^5.$ We shall express all vectors w.r.t. a canonical basis with a typical member $e_A,$ where $A\subseteq\{1,2,3,4,5\}.$ We shall call the vectors multivectors.

Let's create a multivector: 
v=: (3.4;1 3),(6.5; 1 4 5),: (_4; 1 3)
This the multivector $$3.4 e_{\{1,3\}}+6.5 e_{\{1,4,5\}}-4 e_{\{1,3\}}.$$ Notice that $e_{\{1,3\}}$ occurs more than once. We may combine all occurences of it by using the comb function: 
comb v
If a multivector has nonzero coefficients for $e_A$ only when $A$ has a given size, we say that the multivector has grade equal to that given size. For example, $$ 4e_{\{1\}} - 5 e_{\{5\}} $$ has grade 1. A pure multivector of grade $k$ is often called a $k$-vector. A 1-vector is called a vector. A multivector that is not pure is called mixed. Grade extraction means extracting the part of a multivector having a given grade. For instance, if we extract grade 2 of $$3.4 e_{\{1,3\}}+e_{\{4,5\}}+6.5 e_{\{1,4,5\}},$$ we shall get $$3.4 e_{\{1,3\}}+e_{\{4,5\}}.$$ This operation is denoted symbolically by $\langle v\rangle_k.$ In J
