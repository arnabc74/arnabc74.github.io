An example of MCMC

A little theory

While the Gibbs distribution may be complicated, it is easy to compute the conditional distribution of a single pixel given the other pixels.

Example: Suppose we draw one random 3 x 3 image from the Gibbs distribution mentioned in the last page. I show you all its pixels except one (the one marked with a red question mark.)
Clearly, depending on the colour of that pixel we can have the following two images.
We have already computed the probailities of these two images in an earlier page. The probs were 1/Z and exp(2/3)/Z, respectively, where Z was the normalising constant (that we did not care to compute). Elementary probaility now gives the necessary conditional probability as 
         1/Z                     exp(2/3)/Z
   ----------------    and    ---------------- , 
   1/Z + exp(2/3)/Z           1/Z + exp(2/3)/Z
   
   ie, 
        1                  exp(2/3)
   ------------    and   ------------, respectively.
   1 + exp(2/3)          1 + exp(2/3)
Note that Z has cancelled out nicely.

The argument above can be easily generalised. Let W be a random image drawn from a Gibbs distribution. Let X be one pixel. Let W\X denote the collection of al the other pixels. The random variable X can take only two values. Given W\X, the conditional probs are as follows. 

P(X=1|W\X) =
                 exp(-sum(f(1,y)))
   ------------------------------------------- ,
         exp(-sum(f(0,y)))+exp(-sum(f(1,y)))
where the sum is over all y that are neighbours of X.

Perfomance

Geman and Geman proved nice properties of this algorithm. In particular, they showed that as N tends to infinity the distribution of the generated image converges to the Gibbs distribution.

The algorithm is a spcial case of a Markov Chain. Note the contrast between the complexity of the distribution and the simplicity of the algorithm. This contrast is the key idea behind MCMC: it is possible to have simple MCs that converge to complicated distributions. They key idea behind MCMC is as follows. Given a complicated distribution try to get simple Markov Chain that converges to it. We shall now explore this systematically.
