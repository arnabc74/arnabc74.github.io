  1. Classical nonparametric methods:

  2. Computer-intensive methods:
    • Bootstrap
    • Cross-validation and CART
    • Jackknife
  3. Sequential inference:
    • Dynamic programming
    • Sequential testing and SPRT
    • Wald's equations, Wald's approximation and fundamental identity

Reference and textbooks etc

  • All the classnotes are online. These will constitute your main reference material. The lecture notes contain many exercises which will be your assignment problems.
  • For the classical nonparametric methods the book by Hollander and Wolfe is good. It is a fat book, but we shall need only some parts of it.
  • The book by Gibbons and Chakraborty is also a standard reference.
  • For computer-intensive nonparametric methods the book by Efron and Tibshirani is a nice book to read.
  • There are many excellent books on sequential statistics, however almost all of them use measure theoretic notations. So they may not be easily accessible to you. Here are a few standard books on the subject:
    1. Sequntial statistics by Wald: This is a very old book, written by one of the fathers of the subject. It is written informally, makes easy reading, but does not contain modern treatments.
    2. Sequential statistics by Siegmund: Good modern book. But may not be easily accessible to you at this stage.
    3. Great Expectations by Chow , Robbins and Siegmund: Another great book, but makes heavy use of measure theory.
