Relations among modes of convergence

by Marco Taboga, PhD

In the previous lectures, we have introduced several notions of convergence of a sequence of random variables (also called modes of convergence). Almost sure convergence implies convergence in probability

If a sequence of random variables converges almost surely to a random variable , then also converges in probability to .

Proof

See, e.g., Resnick (1999).

Convergence in probability implies convergence in distribution

If a sequence of random variables converges in probability to a random variable , then also converges in distribution to .

Proof

See, for example, Resnick (1999).

Almost sure convergence implies convergence in distribution

If a sequence of random variables converges almost surely to a random variable , then also converges in distribution to .

Proof

This is obtained putting together the previous relations (almost sure convergence implies convergence in probability, which in turn implies convergence in distribution).
