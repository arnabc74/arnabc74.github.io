Dirichlet distribution

Now we are going to work with a multivariate distribution called the Dirichlet distribution, which has is a multivariate generalisation of the Beta distribution.

Definition: Dirichlet distribution We say that $(X_1,...,X_p)$ has Dirichlet distribution with parameters $a_1,...,a_p>0$ if the joint density is $$f(x_1,...,x_p) = \left\{\begin{array}{ll}c x_1^{a_1-1}x_2^{a_2-1}\cdots x_p^{a_p-1}&\text{if }(x_1,...,x_p)\in D\\ 0&\text{otherwise.}\end{array}\right. $$ where $$D = \{(x_1,...,x_p)~:~\forall i~~x_i\geq 0,~~\sum_1^p x_i=1\},$$ and $$c = \frac{\Gamma(a_1+\cdots+a_p)}{\Gamma(a_1)\cdots \Gamma(a_p)}.$$
It is not immediately obvious that the total integral of this function is indeed 1. However, it is easy for $p=2,$ because if $(X_1,X_2)\sim Dir(a_1,a_2)$ then $X_1\sim Beta(a_1,a_2)$ and $X_2 = 1-X_1.$ Starting with this as the basis, we can use induction over $p$ to establish the general case (easy, try it!).

The following properties are all obvious from the definition.
Theorem If $(X_1,X_2,...,X_p)\sim Dir(a_1,a_2,...,a_p)$, then
  1. for any $k\geq 2$ and distinct $i_1,...,i_k\in\{1,...,p\}$ we have $(X_{i_1},...,X_{i_k})\sim Dir(a_{i_1},...,a_{i_k}).$
  2. each $X_i\sim Beta\left(a_i,\sum_{j\neq i} a_j\right).$

We can immediately write down the mean (i.e., expectation) and variance of each $X_i$ from results of Beta distribution.
