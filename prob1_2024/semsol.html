Solution to problem 1

Let $X=$ number of draws needed until first repetion.

Then $X$ takes values 2,3,4,...

Take any $k\in\{2,3,4,...\}.$

The event $\{X=k\}$ occurs in the following steps:
  • Choose first digit: 10 ways
  • Choose next digit avoiding the previous: $(10-1)$ ways
  • Continue like this up to $(k-1)$-st draw.
  • Choose the $k$-th same as the previous: $1$ way.
Total number of ways: $10\times 9^{k-2}\times 1.$

Possibly number of ways to draw the first $k$ digits is $10^k,$ all equally likely.

So $P(X=k) = \frac{10\times 9^{k-2}\times 1}{10^k} = (0.9)^{k-1}0.1.$

Hence $E(X) = \sum_2^ \infty k (0.9)^{k-1}0.1=\cdots.$

Solution to problem 2

$X$ takes the values $-1,0,1,2$ with probabilities $0.1,0.2,0.2,0.5,$ respectively. So, conditional on $X>0,$ it takes the values 1 and 2 with probabilities $\frac{0.2}{0.2+0.5}$ and $\frac{0.5}{0.2+0.5}$.

So $E(X|X>0) = \cdots = \frac{12}{7}.$ Also, $E(X^2|X>0) = \cdots = \frac{22}{7}.$

So $V(X|X>0)=E(X^2|X>0) -E^2(X|X>0) = \cdots.$

But conditional on $X>0,$ the distribution of $X$ is degenerate at $1. $ So $E(X|X>0) = 1.$

Solution to problem 4

$cov(X,Y|Z)=E(XY|Z)-E(X|Z)E(Y|Z).$

So, by tower property, $E(cov(X,Y|Z))=E(XY)-E(E(X|Z)E(Y|Z)).$

Again, $cov(E(X|Z),E(Y|Z))= E(E(X|Z)E(Y|Z))-E(E(X|Z))E(E(Y|Z))=E(E(X|Z)E(Y|Z))-E(X)E(Y),$ by tower property.

Hence $E(cov(X,Y|Z))+cov(E(X|Z),E(Y|Z))= E(XY)-E(X)E(Y)=cov(X,Y),$ as required.

Solution to problem 5

Yes they must be uncorrelated. Since non-degenerate, enough to show $cov(X,Z)=0.$

Now $$\begin{eqnarray*} cov(X,Z) & = & E(XZ)-E(X)E(Z)\\ & = & E(XY)-E(XE(Y|X))-E(X)E(Y-E(Y|X))\\ & = & E(XY)-E(XE(Y|X))-E(X)[E(Y)-E(E(Y|X))]\\ & = & E(XY)-E(XE(Y|X))-E(X)[E(Y)-E(Y)]~~\left[\mbox{by tower property.}\right]\\ & = & E(XY)-E(XE(Y|X))\\ & = & E(XY)-E(E(XY|X))~~\left[\mbox{by substitution property.}\right]\\ & = & E(XY)-E(XY)~~\left[\mbox{by tower property.}\right]\\ & = & 0, \end{eqnarray*}$$ as required.

Solution to problem 6

Part 1

Let $X\sim Geometric(p).$

$P(X\geq n+r|X\geq n)=\frac{P(X\geq n+r, X\geq n)}{P(X\geq n)} = \frac{P(X\geq n+r)}{P(X\geq n)}.$

Now, $\forall k\in{\mathbb N}~~P(X\geq k) = \sum_{i=k}^\infty (1-p)^{i-1}p = (1-p)^{k-1}.$

So $P(X\geq n+r|X\geq n)= (1-p)^r,$ free of $n,$ as required.

Part 2

Let $X$ be ${\mathbb N}$-valued and memoryless.

Let $P(X=i) = p_i$ for $i\in{\mathbb N}.$

Then $\theta = P(X\geq n+1|X\geq n)$ is free of $n.$

Now $P(X\geq 1) = 1$, since $X$ is ${\mathbb N}$-valued.

Again, for $k\geq 2,$ $$\begin{eqnarray*} P(X\geq k) & = & P(X\geq k|X \geq 1)~~\left[\mbox{$\because P(X\geq 1) = 1$}\right]\\ & = & \frac{P(X\geq k)}{P(X\geq 1)}\\ & = & \frac{P(X\geq 2)}{P(X\geq 1)} \times \frac{P(X\geq 3)}{P(X\geq 2)} \times\cdots\times \frac{P(X\geq k)}{P(X\geq k-1)}\\ &= & \theta ^{k-1}. \end{eqnarray*}$$

So $P(X= k)=P(X\geq k)-P(X\geq k+1) = \theta^{k-1}-\theta^k = \theta ^{k-1}(1-\theta).$

So $X\sim Geometric(1-\theta),$ as required.
