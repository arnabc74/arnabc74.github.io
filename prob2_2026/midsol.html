Midsem solution

Question 1

Let $U = X+2Y$ and $V = Y.$

Here $X,Y\in[0,1]$ and so $U\in[0,3].$ Similarly $V\in[0,1].$

We have $X = U-2V$ and $Y = V.$

Thus, the map $(X,Y)\mapsto (U,V)$ is bijective.

Since it is linear, the Jacobian is just the matrix $\left[\begin{array}{ccccccccccc}1 & -2\\0 & 1 \end{array}\right],$ which has determinant 1.

Now the joint density of $(X,Y)$ is $f_{X,Y}(x,y) =\left\{\begin{array}{ll}6&\text{if }(x,y)\in S\\ 0&\text{otherwise.}\end{array}\right., $ since area of $S$ is $\int_0^1 (x-x^2)\, dx = \frac 12-\frac 13 = \frac 16.$

So the joint density of $(U,V)$ is $f_{U,V}(u,v) = f_{X,Y}(u-2v, v) = \left\{\begin{array}{ll}6&\text{if }(u-2v,v)\in S\\ 0&\text{otherwise.}\end{array}\right..$

Let $T = \{(u,v)~:~(u-2v,v)\in S\}.$

$0\leq u-2v \leq 1$ and $(u-2v)^2\leq v \leq u-2v.$

So we have $v\leq \frac u2$, $v\leq \frac u3$, $v\geq \frac{u-1}{2}$ and $(u-2v)^2\leq v.$

Now $u-2v\geq 0,$ and so $u-2v\leq \sqrt v$ or $u\leq 2v+\sqrt v.$

Combining all these we have $v\in [0,1]$ and $u\in[].$
