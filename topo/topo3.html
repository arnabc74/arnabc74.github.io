    EXAMPLE (2022.14):  Prove that the set of components of a topological space forms a partition of that space.[5]

    SOLUTION: Let $(X,\tau)$ be our topological space. We define a binary relation on $X$ as follows.

    For $a,b\in X$ we shall say $a\sim b$ if and only if there are no disjoint clopen sets $A,B\subseteq X$ with $a\in A$ and $b\in B.$

    Clearly, this relation is reflexive and symmetric. Also it is transitive.
    Because: Let $a\sim b$ and $b\sim c.$ Let, if possible, $a\not\sim c.$

    Then there are disjoint clopen $A,C\subseteq X$ with $a\in A$ and $c\in C.$

    But since $a\sim b,$ hence $b\not\in C.$ Also since $b\sim c,$ hence $b\not\in A.$

    EXAMPLE (2023.13a):  Suppose $(X,\tau)$ is a topological space and ${\cal F} = \{F_\alpha~:~\alpha\in \Lambda\}$ ($\Lambda $ is an index set) is any family of closed subsets of $X$ with the property that $\cap_{i=1}^n F_{\alpha_i} \neq \phi$ for any finite subfamily $\{F_{\alpha_i}~:~\alpha_i\in \Lambda,~~i=1,2,...,n\}$ of ${\cal F}.$ Prove that $X$ is compact if and only if $\cap_{\alpha\in \Lambda} F_\alpha\neq\phi.$[3]

    SOLUTION: এই অংকের ভাষাটা একটু গোলমেলে৷ ঠিক করে বলতে গেলে আগে একটা সংজ্ঞা থাকা দরকার--
    আমরা বলব যে, একটা closed set-এর family-র finite intersection property (FIP) আছে, যদি এর মধ্যে যাই finite subfamily নাও না কেন, তার intersection-টা non-empty হবে৷
    এই প্রশ্নে আমাদের দেখাতে বলেছে দুটো জিনিস--
    • এক, যদি একটা compact space-এর মধ্যে FIP-বিশিষ্ট কোনো family নাও, তবে তার intersection-টা non-empty হবেই৷
    • দুই, যদি কোনো topological space-এর বেলায় এমন হয় যে, FIP-বিশিষ্ট যেকোনো family-র intersection-ই non-empty, তবে topological space-টা compact হতে বাধ্য৷
    এটা আসলে compact-এর সংজ্ঞাটাই open-এর বদলএ closed দিয়ে প্রকাশ করা৷ Let the topological space be compact. Take any family ${\cal F}=\{F_ \alpha~:~\alpha\in \Lambda\}$ with FIP. Let, if possible, $\cap _{\alpha\in \Lambda} F_ \alpha = \phi.$ Then, de Morgan, $\cup _{\alpha\in \Lambda} F_ \alpha^c = X.$ So $\{F_ \alpha^c~:~ \alpha\in \Lambda\}$ is an open cover of $X.$

    Since $X$ is compact, there is a finite subcover, $\{F_{\alpha_1}^c,...,F_{\alpha_n}^c\}$ ie, $\cup _{i=1}^n F_ \alpha_i^c = X,$ ie, $\cap _{i=1}^n F_ \alpha_i = \phi (\contra$ FIP$).$

    Conversely, if every family with FIP has non-empty intersection, then we shall show that $X$ must be compact.

    Let $\{U_ \alpha~:~\alpha\in \Xi\}$ be an open cover for $X.$ Then $\cap _{\alpha\in \Xi} U_ \alpha^c = \phi.$

    Hence $\{U_ \alpha^c~:~\alpha\in \Xi\}$ cannot have FIP, ie, we have some finite subfamily $\{U_{\alpha_1}^c,...,U_{\alpha_n}^c\}$ with $\cap _{i=1}^n U_ {\alpha_i}^c = \phi$, ie $\cap _{i=1}^n U_ {\alpha_i} = X.$

    This is a finite subcover of $X.$     ///

    EXAMPLE (2021.13a):  Prove or disprove: The intersection of any family of compact subsets of a space is compact.[3]

    SOLUTION: The statement is false.

    Counterexample: Let $X = {\mathbb N}\cup\{-\infty,\infty\}.$

    Let $\tau = \big\{~\{-\infty\}\cup{\mathbb N},~\{\infty\}\cup{\mathbb N},~\{-\infty,\infty\}\cup{\mathbb N}~\big\}\cup {\mathscr P}({\mathbb N}).$

    Then $\tau$ is a topology on $X.$
    Because: Clearly, $\phi, X\in\tau.$

    Let $\{U_ \alpha~:~ \alpha\in \Lambda\}\subseteq \tau.$ If $\infty$ or $-\infty$ is in some $U_ \alpha,$ then $\cup_ {\alpha\in \Lambda} U_ \alpha$ one of $\{-\infty\}\cup{\mathbb N},$ $\{\infty\}\cup{\mathbb N},$ or $X.$ If $\infty $ or $-\infty$ is not in any of the $U_ \alpha$'s, then $\cup_ {\alpha\in \Lambda} U_ \alpha\subseteq {\mathbb N}.$

    Thus, in either case, $\cup_ {\alpha\in \Lambda} U_ \alpha\in \tau.$

    Let $U_1,U_2\in\tau.$ If $U_1, U_2$ do not contain $\pm\infty,$ then $U_1\cap U_2\subseteq{\mathbb N}.$ If $U_1 $ or $U_2$ equals $X,$ then also done.

    If $-\infty\in U_1$
    Here $K_1 = \{-\infty\}\cup {\mathbb N}$ and $K_2 = \{\infty\}\cup {\mathbb N}$ are both compact. Because any cover of $K_1$ must contain $K_1$ itself. Similarly for $K_2.$

    But $K_1\cap K_2={\mathbb N},$ which is not compact, because the open cover $\big\{~\{n\}~:~n\in{\mathbb N}~\big\}$ does not have a finite subcover.     ///
